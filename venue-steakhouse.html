💡 Fun Facts

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿

Founded by a Scottish butcher and his Romanian partner — the whole restaurant is built around their story. Every photo on the wall has a tale behind it. The owner will probably tell you one whether you ask or not.

🥩

They specialise in Scottish beef — proper dry-aged steaks cooked the way a butcher would cook them. When your founder literally cuts meat for a living, the steak isn't an afterthought.

📍

It's on Carrer de Rosselló, 365 — literally a 10-minute walk from the Eixample flat. You could stumble home. This is by design.

⭐

Rated 4.7 out of 5 on TripAdvisor and ranked in the top 120 of over 9,000 Barcelona restaurants. For a steakhouse in a city obsessed with seafood, that's saying something.

🇷🇴

The menu mixes Scottish and Romanian influences — haggis balls alongside traditional Romanian dishes. It's a love story served on a plate. Literally.

🧠 Trivia

🥩 Steak Quiz

What's the most-ordered cut at The Venue — and why does the owner refuse to serve it well-done?

The ribeye — and he won't serve it well-done because he's a Scottish butcher who actually cares about the meat. Overcook a dry-aged ribeye in front of a butcher and you'll get a lecture, not a steak.

🤔 Quick Quiz

How many restaurants in Barcelona are called "The Venue" — and is this one related to any of them?

Just this one. "The Venue" is its own thing — a family operation run by the Scottish-Romanian couple. The name sounds generic until you realise every "venue" pun in Barcelona now leads here.
