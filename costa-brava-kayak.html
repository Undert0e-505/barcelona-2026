💡 Fun Facts

🛶

You kayak around Sant Feliu de Guíxols and S'Agaró on the Costa Brava — "Wild Coast" in Catalan. The name comes from the rugged cliffs and hidden coves that made it impossible to invade by sea. Perfect for invading by kayak.

🐙

The snorkelling takes place in a marine reserve — expect to see octopus, moray eels, cormorants, and colourful native fish. The Mediterranean might not be the tropics, but the sea life here is surprisingly vivid.

🏔️

The coach drives through the Catalan valleys for about 75 minutes — olive groves, pine forests, and medieval villages. It's basically a free tour of the interior on the way to the coast.

🧊

Guides take you into sea caves along the route — conditions permitting. The water inside glows turquoise. Safety is priority, so no caves if the sea's rough, but when it's calm it's properly magical.

🍻

The trip ends with free beers back in Barcelona. Kayak through caves, snorkel with octopus, chill on white sand, then get a beer. It's the most Catalan activity possible.

🧠 Trivia

🇪🇸 Coast Quiz

What does "Costa Brava" literally mean in Catalan?

"Wild Coast" or "Rugged Coast" — the name was coined in 1908 by journalist Ferran Agulló, who was impressed by the dramatic cliffs and hidden coves. Before tourism, the whole coastline was considered too wild to be useful. Now it's too useful to be wild.

🤔 Quick Quiz

How many sea cave routes does the kayak tour have — and why don't you always get the same one?

Four different routes. The guides pick based on the day's wind, waves, and group ability. No two trips are the same — the Mediterranean decides your adventure. It's choose-your-own-own-ending but the sea is the author.
