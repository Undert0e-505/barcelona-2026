💡 Fun Facts

💧

Built for the 1929 Barcelona International Exposition, the fountain was originally called just "The Magic Fountain" — it literally has magic in the name.

🎵

The show combines water jets, coloured lights, and music — everything from classical to modern pop. The water jets can reach over 50 metres high. Shows run at 21:00 and 21:30, each lasting about 30 minutes.

🏛️

It sits at the foot of Montjuïc, right below the Palau Nacional (the grand building at the top of the steps). The view looking down towards Plaça d'Espanya is the classic shot.

🏜️

It was shut for years due to drought restrictions. Barcelona only reopened it after the water situation improved. Old blogs still say it's closed — ignore them.

👀

The best viewing spots are on the steps looking down towards Plaça d'Espanya. Slightly off-axis along Avinguda Maria Cristina is less packed but still great.
