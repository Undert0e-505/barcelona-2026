💡 Fun Facts

🏗️

Construction began in 1882 — that's 143 years ago and counting. Gaudí took over the project in 1883 and devoted the last 43 years of his life to it exclusively. When asked why it was taking so long, he said: "My client is not in a hurry."

⛪

It will have 18 towers when complete — 12 for the apostles, 4 for the evangelists, 1 for the Virgin Mary, and the central tower for Jesus Christ at 172.5 metres, making it the tallest church in the world. Currently 8 towers are finished.

🌳

The interior is designed to feel like a stone forest. The columns branch like trees, the ceiling resembles a canopy of leaves, and the stained glass changes colour through the day — warm on one side (sunrise), cool on the other (sunset).

💀

Gaudí died in 1926 after being hit by a tram on his way to prayer. He was dressed so shabbily that taxi drivers refused to take him, assuming he was a beggar. He's now buried in the crypt of the very church he designed.

🔢

Every surface has meaning. The Nativity Facade (east, Gaudí's work) celebrates birth with organic forms. The Passion Facade (west, later sculptors) is angular and harsh, representing suffering. The Glory Facade (south, still building) will represent the path to God.

🧠 Trivia

⛪ Architecture Quiz

How is the Sagrada Família funded — and why does that matter for the completion date?

Entirely by private donations and ticket sales. No government money, no church funding. This is why it's taken 143 years — they only build when they can afford to. Current estimates put completion around 2026–2032, but Gaudí himself said "My client is not in a hurry," so who's counting?

🤔 Quick Quiz

What did the anarchists do to Gaudí's models during the Spanish Civil War?

Burned them. In 1936, anarchists set fire to Gaudí's workshop in the crypt, destroying his original plaster models, drawings, and notes. The current architects have spent decades reconstructing his intentions from fragments, photographs, and Gaudí's own cryptic annotations. The rebuilding of the models took longer than some phases of the actual building.
