💡 Fun Facts

💥

Built in 1937–1938 during the Spanish Civil War to defend Barcelona from Franco's bombing raids. The guns here shot at real planes dropping real bombs.

🏙️

At 257 metres up, you can see the entire city: Sagrada Família, Torre Agbar, the coast, and Montjuïc. On a clear day you can see the Pyrenees.

🏘️

After the war, the bunkers became a shanty town — families who'd fled the bombing moved into the ruins and stayed until the 1990s when the city relocated them.

📷

For decades this was a locals-only secret. Instagram changed that — it's now one of the most photographed spots in Barcelona. Go early or go late to beat the crowds.

🚫

There are no bars, no toilets, no bins at the top. It's raw concrete ruins on a hill. Bring everything you need and take your rubbish home.

🧠 Trivia

🇪🇸 History Quiz

How many anti-aircraft batteries did Barcelona have during the Civil War?

Four. The Carmel battery is the only one you can still visit. The others were at Montjuïc, Turó de la Peira, and the port area. Most were demolished or buried under later construction.

🤔 Quick Quiz

What's the actual name of the hill the bunkers sit on?

Turó de la Rovira. Everyone calls them "Bunkers del Carmel" after the neighbourhood below, but the hill itself is Turó de la Rovira. Pedants rejoice.
