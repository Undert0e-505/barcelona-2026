💡 Fun Facts

🍫

Carrer de Petritxol has been Barcelona's "Chocolate Street" since the 18th century — granjas and xocolateries have lined it for over 250 years.

📖

The name Dulcinea comes from Don Quixote — she was the knight-errant's imaginary lady love. The original owner clearly had literary taste.

🥛

A "granja" literally means farm — these cafés started as milk bars where you'd get fresh dairy with your chocolate. The tradition dates back to when Barcelona had actual cows nearby.

☕

The hot chocolate here is suisa style — so thick your spoon stands up in it. It's basically drinking a melted chocolate bar. No complaints.

🏆

Granja Dulcinea has been serving continuously since 1941 — surviving the Franco era, the 1992 Olympics boom, and millions of Instagram tourists. The churros don't care about your followers.

🧠 Trivia

🍫 Chocolate Challenge

How many granjas (traditional chocolate cafés) still survive on Carrer de Petritxol today?

Three! Granja Dulcinea, Granja La Pallissa, and Granja M. La Xocolateria. Once there were many more — the street was wall-to-wall chocolate. Hard times.

🤔 Quick Quiz

What makes a "suís" (Swiss) hot chocolate different from the regular kind at a granja?

The suís has whipped cream on top! Regular is just the thick chocolate. The name supposedly comes from Swiss tourists who couldn't handle it without cream. Weak.
