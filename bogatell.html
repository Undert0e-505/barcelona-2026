💡 Why Bogatell?

🏖️

Bogatell is the locals' favourite — cleaner, wider and less crowded than Barceloneta. Revamped for the 1992 Olympics, it's got golden sand, clear water, and a proper neighbourhood feel.

🚇

L4 (Yellow Line) to Bogatell station — then a 10-minute walk through Poblenou. Grab a coffee on Rambla del Poblenou on the way.

🏐

Free volleyball courts right on the beach — just walk up and join a game. Bogatell is the volleyball hub of Barcelona.

🏃

The seafront promenade stretches for miles — perfect for a run, a stroll, or renting a bike. Calisthenics and fitness zones along the way.

☀️

Minimal shade on the beach — bring a hat, sunscreen, or rent a sun lounger. The sun in late May is intense.

🎯 Activities & Things To Do

Stand-up paddleboard lessons and hire right on the beach at La Mar Bella (next to Bogatell). Lessons from €35. Sunrise sessions available from 8am. Great for beginners — the Med is calm in May.

💡 Practical Tips

🚇

Getting there: Metro L4 (Yellow) to Bogatell station. Exit onto Carrer de Pujades, walk 10 mins east through Poblenou to the beach. Or take the same line to Llacuna — slightly closer.

👜

Valuables: Don't leave bags unattended on the beach. Take turns swimming so someone's always watching your stuff. A waterproof phone pouch is worth having.

🧴

Sunscreen is not optional. May sun in Barcelona is surprisingly fierce. SPF 50, reapply after swimming. The beach has minimal natural shade.

🍽️

Spanish lunch hours: Most restaurants don't open until 13:00–13:30 and close around 16:00. Plan your lunch timing, or eat at the chiringuito which serves all day.

💧

Bring water. There are fountains along the promenade, but a bottle saves endless trips. The beach kiosks charge tourist prices.
