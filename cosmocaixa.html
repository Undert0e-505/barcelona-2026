💡 Fun Facts

🌋

The building has a real dead volcano in the basement. A recreated geological formation with running water that shows erosion, sedimentation, and volcanic activity. William and Matthew can walk through it. It's genuinely impressive, not just for kids.

🌴

The Inundated Forest is a 1,000m² replica of a section of Amazon rainforest — complete with real trees, running water, 100+ species of plants, capybaras, piranhas, anacondas, and tropical birds. It's not a display — it's an actual ecosystem.

🔬

Over 100 interactive exhibits across physics, mechanics, optics, and sound. Everything is hands-on — push buttons, pull levers, spin things, make noise. Designed for kids to wreck and adults to pretend they're not enjoying.

🏛️

Originally the Casa de la Ciència (House of Science), it was built in 1904 as a modernist building by Josep Domènech i Estapà. The "CosmoCaixa" name comes from the 2004 renovation by La Caixa bank who spent €70 million turning it into one of Europe's best science museums.

🆓

General entry is free. Only the planetarium, temporary exhibitions, and some workshops cost extra. First Sunday of every month, everything is free including the planetarium. For our trip, general entry is free any day.

🧠 Trivia

🔬 Science Quiz

How many animal species live in the Inundated Forest exhibit?

Over 100 species. Including capybaras (the world's largest rodent), piranhas, anacondas, tropical fish, and free-flying birds. It's the only place in Barcelona where you can watch a capybara eat lettuce while a piranha watches from the next tank.

🤔 Quick Quiz

What famous scientist's brain is preserved and on display at CosmoCaixa?

Santiago Ramón y Cajal. The Nobel Prize-winning neuroscientist's brain slices are part of the permanent collection. He's the father of modern neuroscience — basically the Spanish Darwin but for brains. His drawings of neurons are still used in textbooks today.
