💡 Fun Facts

🐉

The roof is shaped like a dragon's back — the colourful round tiles are its scales. The turret with the cross is St George's lance piercing the dragon.

🦴

Locals call it "Casa dels Ossos" (House of Bones) because the ground-floor balconies look like skulls and the pillars look like bones.

🌊

Gaudí hated straight lines — he said there are none in nature. Every surface curves, waves, or spirals.

💎

The facade uses trencadís — broken ceramic tiles sourced from local factories. Gaudí turned industrial waste into art.

🏗️

Originally built in 1877, Gaudí renovated it in 1904 for Josep Batlló. Neighbours thought he was mad. It's now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

🧠 Trivia

🇪🇸 Barcelona Brain Teaser

Casa Batlló is one of how many Gaudí buildings on Passeig de Gràcia?

Two! Casa Batlló at number 43 and Casa Milà (La Pedrera) at number 92. Both UNESCO sites, both absolutely bonkers.
