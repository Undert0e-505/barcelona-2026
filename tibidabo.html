💡 Fun Facts

🎠

Opened in 1899, it's one of the oldest amusement parks in the world still operating. Some of the original rides are still running. The vintage carousel has been spinning for over 120 years.

⛪

The Temple Expiatori del Sagrat Cor church sits right on top of the mountain next to the park — a giant stone church with a golden Jesus statue visible from across Barcelona. Yes, roller coasters and religion, side by side.

🏔️

At 512 metres, the views from the top beat every rooftop bar in the city. You can see the entire coastline, Montserrat mountains, and on a clear day, the Pyrenees.

🎬

The park appeared in Woody Allen's "Vicky Cristina Barcelona" — the scene with the overhead views. It's also where Barcelona goes on dates. Very romantic, if you don't mind heights.

✝️

The mountain's name comes from the Bible — "tibi dabo" is Latin for "I will give to you," words the devil supposedly used when tempting Jesus from a high mountain. Barcelona built a church and a theme park on the spot. On brand.

🧠 Trivia

🎢 Park Quiz

Tibidabo opened in 1899. Which is the only European amusement park that's older and still operating?

Tivoli Gardens in Copenhagen — opened in 1843. Tibidabo is the second-oldest in Europe. Not bad for a park on top of a mountain that takes three transport connections to reach.

🤔 Quick Quiz

What does "Tibidabo" literally mean — and why is that slightly unhinged for a family attraction?

"I will give to you" — the devil's words to Jesus during the temptation on the mountain. So Barcelona named its beloved family amusement park after Satan's pitch. The church next door was probably not a coincidence.
